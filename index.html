Welcome to the Paradise of the North

Batanes is the northernmost and smallest province in the Philippines, characterized by its majestic rolling hills, steep cliffs, and vast pasture lands. Unlike the tropical palm-fringed beaches common in the rest of the country, Batanes offers a rugged landscape reminiscent of the Scottish Highlands. It is home to the Ivatan people, a resilient and honest community whose culture is deeply rooted in harmony with nature and the elements.

Dubbed as a "Nature's Playground", the archipelago province has been declared by the government as a Protected Landscape and Seascape because of its rare flora and fauna, undulating terrain, diverse marine life, limestone cliffs, and panoramic sceneries. it boasts of stretches of white and cream colored sandy beaches and clear water.

Discover the untouched beauty of the Philippines with a trip to breathtaking Batanes. Immerse yourself in a world of stunning landscapes, crystal clear waters, and rich culture. Don't miss out on this once-in-a-lifetime adventure - book your trip to Batanes now and let the beauty take your breath away!

Attractions

Basco Lighthouse

Located on Naidi Hills, this 66-foot lighthouse offers a 360-degree view of Mt. Iraya, the West Philippine Sea, and the town of Basco. It stands on the site of a former American telegraph tower destroyed during WWII. It is a famous six-story lighthouse located on Naidi Hill in Batanes, built to guide seafarers safely back to shore. Surrounded by rich history and stunning landscapes, the lighthouse offers breathtaking views of the town, mountains, and sea. Today, it serves as both a working lighthouse and one of the most visited tourist attractions in Batanes.

Valugan Boulder Beach

A breathtaking coastline covered in smooth, massive boulders instead of sand. These stones were spewed by Mt. Iraya during its eruption in 400 AD and have been polished for centuries by the powerful waves of the Pacific Ocean. It is a famous rocky shoreline in Batanes formed by volcanic eruptions and shaped by the powerful waves of the Pacific Ocean. Its smooth boulders, dramatic cliffs, and cool sea breeze create a peaceful yet breathtaking view. The beach is known as one of the most iconic natural attractions in Batanes because of its unique beauty and relaxing atmosphere.

Tukon Chapel (Mt. Carmel Chapel)

A stone-made chapel perched on a hill, inspired by traditional Ivatan stone houses. Its walls are made of boulders, and the interior features hand-painted saints created by local artists, offering a serene and spiritual atmosphere. This is also known as Tukon Church, is a beautiful stone chapel in Batanes designed after a traditional Ivatan house. Located on top of a hill, it offers breathtaking views of the rolling landscapes, the Pacific Ocean, and the West Philippine Sea. Despite being damaged by a strong typhoon in 2016, the chapel remains a symbol of faith, resilience, and Ivatan culture.

Savidug Village

A heritage village on Sabtang Island that serves as a living museum of Ivatan history. It features rows of "Sinadumparan" stone houses with thick walls and cogon roofs designed to withstand the strongest typhoons. A historic village in Sabtang Island that reflects the early lifestyle and traditions of the Ivatan people. Known for its traditional stone houses, peaceful community, and scenic hills, the village showcases the rich culture and history of Batanes. Visitors can also explore the nearby Savidug Idiang and St. Thomas Aquinas Chapel, which add to the village’s cultural and historical charm.

Honesty Coffee Shop

A world-famous unmanned store in Ivana. There are no cashiers or guards; customers simply pick what they need, record it in a logbook, and drop their payment in a wooden box. It stands as a monument to the integrity of the Batanes people. A unique café in Batanes known for operating without staff and relying entirely on the honesty of its customers. Visitors simply take what they need and place their payment in designated boxes, reflecting the strong values of trust and integrity within the community. Located near the Port of Ivana, the shop has become a popular tourist attraction and a symbol of the peaceful and honest culture of Batanes.

Travel Guide

The fastest and most convenient way to travel to Batanes from mainland Luzon is by air. Philippine Airlines operates flights from Manila through NAIA and from Clark International Airport going to Basco Airport, the main airport of Batanes. The flight from Manila to Basco usually takes around 1 hour and 45 minutes. Travelers may also fly from Tuguegarao to Basco through SkyPasada.

Estimated Airfare to Batanes:

Manila to Basco (one-way): around ₱8,000–₱15,000 during regular booking periods

Clark to Basco (one-way): approximately ₱10,000–₱15,000

Cagayan to Basco (one-way): about ₱7,000

Promo fares and seat sales may go as low as ₱4,000–₱5,000 when booked early

Travel Tip

It is recommended to reserve flights in advance and plan your trip during the dry season, especially from March to May, to experience better weather conditions and avoid possible flight cancellations.
